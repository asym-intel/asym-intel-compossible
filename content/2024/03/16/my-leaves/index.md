> _' I feel as if I'm losing all my leaves. The branches, and the wind, and the rain. I don't know what's happening anymore.' -_ Anthony ('The Father')

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/tree.webp)

* * *

Struggling wordlessly into the light

Startling eruption of limb

As green as green has ever been

Flowing and fumblingly growing 

Out of dark moist cloister

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/as-green-as-green.webp)

* * *

> Hail Mary 
> 
> Blessed is the fruit 
> 
> Of thy womb
> 
> Pray for us now1
> 
> * * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/hail-mary.webp)

* * *

We are born 

Sun-seekers

Gargantuan

Being-eaters

To be eaten

Composable

Cooperation of cell

Atomistic apostles of Aten

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/son-of-aten.webp)

* * *

> Those on Earth come from your hand as you made them
> 
> When you have dawned they live
> 
> When you [have gone] they die
> 
> You yourself are lifetime, one lives by you
> 
> All eyes are on your beauty until you set
> 
> All labour ceases when you rest in the west2
> 
> * * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/pray-to-aten.webp)

* * *

Deliciously off-kilter

Younglings 

Rare and delicate 

Star-fire flowers

Bestowers of heart

Murderous and monstrous 

Little _mesdames et messieurs_

_Wyrdlings_  of woe and wonder

Gigglous adorations

Time’s incarnations 

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/star-flowers.webp)

> * * *
> 
> An ash I know, Yggdrasil its name,
> 
> With water white is the great tree wet;
> 
> Thence come the dews that fall in the dales,
> 
> Green by Urth's well does it ever grow.
> 
> …Laws they made there, and life allotted
> 
> To the sons of men, and set their fates.3
> 
> * * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/yggdrasil.webp)

* * *

Each and every 

A-symmetry 

Or fault

Is ready to take that shape

And form uniquely our own

If only we can survive 

The violence 

Of glorious

Juvenescence

That most exciting journey to behold 

For the gods and the old

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/violence-of-juvenescence.webp)

* * *

_The Spirit is not born and does not die  _

_It does not come into being or cease  _

_It is not destroyed with the passing of the body_

_So why do you cry?_4

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/why-do-you-cry.webp)

* * *

Broadening boughs 

Pushing down roots 

Weathering wizening

Procreators 

Strange standing waves

Beautifully cracked 

Hallow vessels

That sing when touched

Strengthening to girth

Heavier crowns

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/strange-standing-waves.webp)

* * *

Good God 

Grace a’me

Bless mine 

Hold time

Momentarily 

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2025/10/anthony.webp)

* * *

Quick spinning seasons

Where everything speeds

Achingly open spaces

Free for next year's trees

Identities distilled 

To essence

Sapping slowly 

Dissolving memory

Hinting of patiently

Waiting eternity

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/dissolving-memory.avif)

_All living things decay_

_They cannot stay._

_Be  _

_Carefully_

_To gain your_

_Salvation._

_Cleave_

_The bonds_

_That bind._

_Reflect, refract_

_The undying light_5 _ _

> * * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/no-staying-grief.webp)

* * *

Though we may grieve

There is no staying grief

We are life’s loves

Labours lost

That leaf 

Then leave

* * *

![](/images/uploads/2024/03/tree-of-life.avif)

* * *

_This poem is dedicated to Sir Anthony Hopkins._

> * * *
