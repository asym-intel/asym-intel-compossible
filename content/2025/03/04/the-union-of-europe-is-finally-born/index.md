> Makes the voice grow hoarse. We  
> Who wished to lay the foundation for gentleness  
> Could not ourselves be gentle._"
> 
> (trans. Scott Horton)

**Albert Camus** was a French philosopher, author, dramatist, journalist and political activist. He was the recipient of the 1957 Nobel Prize in Literature at the age of 44. He took an active role in the underground resistance movement in France. In this quote, Camus reminds us that freedom requires some inequality (useful inequalities), whereas justice requires that inequality to be constrained, otherwise freedom is empty for those without power or sufficient resources. That is the essence of what I mean when I say our form of capitalism must change: to provide more freedom to the majority and more sustainable measures of economic success. Extract from the long essay 'The Rebel'.
